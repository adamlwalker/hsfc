HSFC Mission Statement

The mission of the Humane Society of Fairfax County, Inc. is to promote humane education; to prevent all forms of cruelty to animals, both domestic and wild, by every legitimate means; and to assist the community with all matters pertaining to the welfare of animals.



Frequently Asked Questions



What hours are you open?

Our administrative offices and cat/small mammal facilities in Fairfax are open to the public Monday through Friday from 10:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m., open Saturday from 10:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m., and closed on Sunday. Our facility in Centreville, which houses our dogs and horses, is open to the public by appointment only.



I need to give up my companion animal.
Can you take him or her?

We are limited with respect to the number of animals we can accommodate and may or may not have space at the time of your call. If space is available, we would be happy to help you find a new home for your animal. If our facilities are full, we can provide you with contact information of other rescue organizations in our area who may have space available.



Where are your offices, animals and thrift store located?

Our administrative office is located at 4057 Chain Bridge Road in Fairfax City across the street from the Fairfax County Courthouse. Our cats, rabbits, small mammals and birds are housed in our Fairfax location. Dogs and horses are housed at our farm in Centreville. You must have an appointment prior to seeing animals at our Centreville location (see below). Our thrift store, Second Chance, is located in Fairfax next door to our administrative office.



How do I become a volunteer?

Please go to the volunteer section of our web site at www.hsfc.org. Browse the site as it contains most of what you will need to know including descriptions of activities and opportunities, and necessary forms. Once you have reviewed the site and determined that HSFC is where you would like to volunteer, please download the appropriate application. Upon receipt of your completed and signed application and agreement, the documents will be processed and you will receive a welcome packet via email.



How old do you have to be to volunteer?

Our volunteers must be 18 or older to be considered regular volunteers. We do accept and welcome minor volunteers age 15 to 18; however, they must be accompanied by a parent or guardian during all service hours. Please check our web site at www.hsfc.org under Volunteer, Kidsâ€™ Activities for suggested activities for youth volunteers under age 15.



Do you offer free or discount spay/neutering and vaccinations for pets?

No, not at this time. We do have resources we can refer you to by calling us at 703-385-7387. The Fairfax County Animal Shelter also offers discount dog and cat spay /neutering and rabies vaccinations for Fairfax County residents. You may contact them at 703-830-1100.



What is the first step if I want to adopt an animal?

The first step is to fill out an application! For cats and other animals located in Fairfax, you may fill out the application online or visit our office to complete an application. For dogs and horses, please fill out or download an application online at www.hsfc.org. Once we receive your application, we will contact you to set up an appointment. If you do not have access to a computer, please call us at 703-385-7387.



I submitted my animal adoption application. What is the process?

One of our employees will contact you to discuss your application if you have submitted it online, usually within 48 hours. If you wish to see cats, rabbits, other small mammals or birds, you may stop by our offices in Fairfax at any time during business hours to discuss your application and/or view the animals. Our dogs and horses are shown strictly by appointment only and you must first speak to one of our employees to arrange an appointment prior to seeing these animals.



Are you connected with the Fairfax County Animal Shelter? What is their phone number and where are they located?

No, we are a privately funded, non-profit 501(c)(3) rescue organization and not affiliated with the Fairfax County Animal Shelter. The Fairfax County Animal Shelter is a county-funded public shelter located at 4500 West Ox Road in Fairfax. Their main number is 703-830-1100.



Do you take donations? What kind do you take or need?

Yes! We do take donations of animal supplies and food. Food donations must be unopened and not have expired Sell By dates. These items may be delivered to our administrative office building at 4057 Chain Bridge Road in Fairfax. Our thrift store, next door to our administrative office, accepts donations of clothing, household items, books, toys, and other small items. Due to space limitations, we cannot accept furniture, mattresses, gym equipment, large electronics, etc.
